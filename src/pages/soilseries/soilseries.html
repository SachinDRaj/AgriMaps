Soil Series

pH

This indicates a soil's level of acidity or alkalinity. Soils with pH below 4.5 are considered very acidic, those with pH values above 8.0 are strongly alkaline and those near a pH of 7.0 are neutral.

CEC

CEC (Cation Exchange Capacity) gives an indication of a soil's ability to store positively charged nutrients like Calcium and Magnesium.

Sand %

This represents the proportion of coarse grains in a soil.

Clay %

This represents the proportion of fine particles in a soil.

P

P (Phosphorous) is a primary plant nutrient required for photosynthesis, root formation and growth.

K

K (Potassium) is a primary plant nutrient needed for photosynthesis and protein formation.

EC

EC (Electrical Conductivity) gives an indication of the amount of soluble salts present in a soil.

Mg

Mg (Magnesium) is a secondary nutrient which plays a central role in chlorophyll formation required for photosynthesis, enzyme activation and plant respiration.

Ca

Ca (Calcium) is a secondary plant nutrient which serves to promote root and leaf development, activate plant enzymes and strengthens cell walls.
